Welcome to Tiritiri Matangi


Overview

Tiritiri Matangi is an open nature reserve just an hour away from Auckland in the Hauraki Gulf of New Zealand. This island is full of a range of wildlife living their best life.

The island is 2.2 square kilometres, managed by the Suppers of Tiritiri Matangi Incorporated, while being supervised by the Department of Conservation. Bird life such as takahē, North Island kōkako, brown teal, saddleback, stitchbird and kiwi attracts between 30,000 and 32,000 visitors per year.

Since 1971, volunteers have planted 280,000 pōhutukawa and other native plants. Tiritiri Matangi Island is also recognised as an official example of success in the United Nations Decade on Ecosystem Restoration.
